# What it needed

A 22-acre regenerative land project in Elgin, Texas. The vision was clear: food forest, off-grid infrastructure, and a live-learn-do model for the surrounding community. But vision isn't fundable. What was missing was the layer between dream and capital — a pitch deck that could walk into a room, a pro-forma that showed the math, a design identity that communicated seriousness.

That's where I came in.

---

## The work

**Pitch deck.** Thirty-one slides. The Reimagine Ranch Master Deck covers the land thesis, the revenue model, the phased build-out, atmospheric water generation, rainwater and cistern systems, the Colorado River water rights picture, and waste management. Every slide built around a single design system — Cormorant Garamond serif headers, warm cream and dark navy surfaces, a consistent visual language that says *we've thought this through*.

**Pro-forma.** A working financial model projecting the project across construction phases. Not a guess — a document that earns the right to sit across from an investor.

**Website.** A public-facing presence that connects land, vision, and people. Built to open relationships, not just describe a project.

**Design system.** Ink/gold/parchment palette. Cathedral gradient. Cormorant Garamond + Jost typefaces. A system that holds across every deliverable — so when the deck, the site, and the one-pager all land on the same desk, they say the same thing.

---

## What moved

The pitch deck shipped and was sent to investors and partners in June 2026. The site is being built. The design system is documented and stable enough for collaborators to use independently.

More to document as the next phases unfold.

---

## What I learned

A land project and a startup pitch deck share more DNA than you'd expect. The challenge is the same: compress a complex, multi-year vision into something that can be understood in 90 seconds and believed in 20 minutes.

The design system solved a problem I didn't fully anticipate. Without a shared visual language, every new deliverable starts from scratch — and starts to drift. Doing the design work first meant every subsequent document arrived already looking like it belonged.

Forward deployment is about arriving before the organization is ready for you. The gap between what Reimagine Ranch needs and what Reimagine Ranch currently has is exactly where the work lives.
